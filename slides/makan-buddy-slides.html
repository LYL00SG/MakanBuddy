The Problem

“Where should I eat?”

  • Singapore has thousands of hawker stalls, food courts, cafes, restaurants and street-food spots — and the scene changes constantly (places open and close).
  • The best pick depends on your location (area/MRT), your craving, and your dietary needs — halal, vegetarian, no pork.
  • Generic search and maps overwhelm; you just want a short, trustworthy shortlist.
  • Who it's for: anyone in Singapore who's hungry and can't decide where to makan.

Why AI is the right tool

A conversation handles vague requests, asks one smart follow-up, remembers context, and synthesises live web results into a concise, sourced shortlist — something a static list or keyword search can't do.

What I Learned

Keeping an AI honest is the real work

Harder than expected

The model happily fabricated source links, ratings, even halal tags. Fixed it with a structured-output schema, prompt rules (“leave empty if you can't confirm”), and code-side validation that drops fake URLs.

Do differently

Start web-search-first from day one. I began dataset-first and had to re-architect mid-project to deliver live, current results.

What's next

Integrate the Google Places API for verified hours, a true “Open now” flag, and real photos of each place.
